A possession starts when a team gains control of the ball and ends with a turnover, shot attempt or any free throws except for technicals.

Point Per Possession takes into consideration shooting results, turnovers and free throws connected to the end of any possession.

As an example, if a player is responsible for the end of three of their team's possessions resulting in 1.) a turnover 2.) a made 3 point field goal and 3.) is fouled in the act of shooting a 2 point shot and the shot and the free throw are both made, then the player has scored 6 points in three possessions which results in a Points Per Possession value of 2.0.

PPP = Total Points Per Possession / Total Possessions
